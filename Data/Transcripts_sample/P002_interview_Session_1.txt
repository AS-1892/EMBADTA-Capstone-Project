PARTICIPANT: The PDF integration is fundamentally broken for financial statements. It doesn't preserve formatting from Excel—columns misalign, formulas disappear. That's unacceptable in my line of work. Also, the search function can't find text within attached documents, which makes client file retrieval slower than my old folder system. And there's no audit trail for who changes what in shared financial models—that's a compliance risk.

INTERVIEWER: How does Productiva Pro compare to productivity tools you've used previously?

PARTICIPANT: Asana was simpler but adequate. This feels like they added features for the sake of features. Do I need a "mood tracker" on my task list? No. Do I need AI suggesting I take breaks? I'm an accountant during tax season—I don't get breaks. The automation could be useful... if it understood GAAP principles. But it doesn't, so I can't trust it for anything material. It's a toy for creative types, not a tool for serious professionals.

INTERVIEWER: What has been most challenging to learn or use?

INTERVIEWER: What has been most challenging to learn or use?

PARTICIPANT: The entire philosophy of it. It's built for flexible, creative work. Accounting is regimented, compliance-driven, sequential. In Asana, I could say "B cannot start until A is complete and reviewed." Here, it's just linear order. That doesn't work for audit preparations where steps have strict prerequisites.

Clients can upload documents directly to their folder instead of emailing. That reduces security risks. And the automated reminders for missing documents work decently. But these are minor conveniences, not worth the disruption to my established systems.
